What is Instagram?

Instagram is a very quick and smart way to take pictures with your mobile phone (primarily) and share them with an already robust community of people doing the same. There are some quick built-in filters to give your photos a charming vintage look. Right now Instagram exists only as an iPhone app, but will soon cross over to other phones and also have a web-based face to it.

Why do I love it?

I carry my phone with me everywhere I go (even to bed!) And now my phone is a tool of creation — anywhere! When I go places I look around for interesting things to capture. Its almost like a journal, I upload and share with my friends and they with me. It’s a fun, interesting way to keep up with those you want to. No to mention that because the community at large is so … large, you can get a peak into events happening all over the world. I get scenes of the Autumn in Georgia (colors we don’t get there in the desert,) and the skyscrapers in Tokyo. It really is something amazing.

How can it be improved?

It’s no secret that I love Instagram. But there is definitely room for improvement. One key point of it’s success is that the service is quick and simple, but I am convinced that a few small tweaks could make all the difference and not infringe on what makes it already a great app/service/community. Here are a few thoughts:

Finding photos I like

Finding photos I have previously “liked” is the most frustrating part of instagram. Perhaps a “liked photos” option might serve us well, it could be in the profile tab next to “your photos.” Other possibilities are to give the option to see all the photos I’ve commented on.

increase the connection

Recent improvements notify us when someone @mentions us or comments on a post we commented on. Other ideas to increase personal connections could be: to allow us to repost other’s work to our viewers, or “photo-reply” (similar to a rebound on dribbble.)

What matters to me

I will freely admit that I want to have my photos on the coveted “popular” page. But wouldn’t it be great if we could filter the popular photos based on what matters to us? Why not see what is popular among those we follow, or our followers — or even me!

define the style

For how great the service is, it’s a visual mess. The app UI is fairly standard, opportunities for originality are lost in places like the “share” tab’s camera, and the profile summaries. I’ve mocked a possible camera back here: it takes hints from Leica’s M8 camera back. Sexy.

Brand standards

The brand itself is suffering the most; I’ve counted 4 logos used in different places and mostly stock UI elements throughout the app. Nothing fancy, or processor intensive is required, just a little brand definition, and consistency. (And please choose this one!)
