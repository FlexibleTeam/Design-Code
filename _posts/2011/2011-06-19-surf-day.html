Surf Day

The concept of surfing is not natural to me. I grew up in the desert. The only water sport we knew as children was squirting each other with the garden hose, trying to prevent ourselves from drying up and withering away in the ridiculously hot sun.

But here I am now, a Californian. The desert is behind me, the hot dry summers of scrambling to get indoors to stand next to the over-taxed air conditioners are a thing of the past. As a Californian, I must surf. And surf I did.

My buddy Sam asked if I was down to go on a day trip to Pacifica to catch some waves and get some tacos. In my most Californian accent I replied "Sure, Bro." — and off we were. It was a beautiful day, sun shining, waves crashing, gulls gulling and my rental-board, "Big Red," tucked under my arm. The water was perfect — until I stepped into it. Freezing cold. Welcome to Northern California.

I paddled out and awkwardly maneuvered myself into position. Ready for my first wave, stomach to the length of the board, I turned my head to see it coming. Water surging all round me, pushing me forward, sea foam leaping up around my ears as the board picked up speed on the wave. I oriented myself enough to pop up onto my feet, the board was more wobbly than I thought it would be. The ocean pushing me back toward the sandy beach, I relished in 5 complete seconds of surfing bliss. The force of the wave died out and the ride was over, I fell off my board as the wave lost it's strength. But I had done it, I surfed.
