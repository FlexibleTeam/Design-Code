###You've Changed

When I was nineteen someone very close to me said something very hurtful to me. “I don't know man, you've changed.” This insight was offered to me in the context of an answer to the question of why we had been spending less and less time together, why we were drifting apart.

I've never forgotten that moment, those words. I carried that hurt with me for a long time.

These are words that are intended to offend. The insult comes from the idea that I, as a person, am not a unique identity. That my personality is so weak to begin with, that it will bend with the changing of the wind. I have no substance, nothing to contribute.

But as with all things, time has a way of lending new perspectives, and I have learned a thing or two about myself. Those words don't hurt anymore. I can see now that they were a compliment (although certainly not intended to be so.)

A person who consciously changes is a person who can adapt. They have to be strong in their sense of self, their core identity must remain intact while they are responding to outside stimuli. They must be aware of themselves and their surroundings, on a micro, and macro level. For a person to not only survive, but to succeed in this ever-changing world they must consistently offer new value, constantly contribute.

Life is about change, it's about growth. It's about advancement and improvement. What kind of man would I be if I did not actively seek to better myself? I would be a failure. I would fall behind and become a footnote in someone else's grand story.

So what does change really mean? It means success over time.

How does one change? I'm not sure I have that answer, I am not sure there is one universal answer. But I know that it is important to always be ready for change, to embrace it and use it as an opportunity to improve yourself and station in life. If you resist change you lose. If you are ready for it when it happens (and it will happen) you will succeed.
