This child here;
he has so much potential
hidden under need.
Pick me up.
Whipe my ass. Feed me.
Life so far.
You can walk
but you can never leave.
I’m not looking.
Goodbye house,
I am going away now,
goodbye dad.
Dancing in the spring
lost in the tall grass
those silly days.
These are mine
you can not have them.
get your own.
I was sick;
had to do extra homework.
Yes, I cried.
Look at you,
blonde hair in your eyes
You see me?
We are funny.
We always like to laugh,
tell me jokes.
I think about
all the things that we
could yet become.
Tell me again;
tell me that I’m not
what you want.
I’m bigger now.
I am just like you.
I’ll follow you.
We are brothers.
We will always be friends;
more than that.
What the hell?
No one told me about
the strange things!
This is trouble.
This will stay with me,
it is unavoidable.
Close my eyes,
the world fades to black,
I’m asleep now.
Let me see
in to those dark places.
We are blind.
This is rage!
Take what mess we have;
make it beautiful.
The morning breaks.
I do love these stories.
A new secret.
Cleaned up now;
we all wear suits and
make good choices.
I can help!
I can do good and
make a difference.
I’m back again.
I don’t fit in anywhere,
so I’ll leave.
Young and silly;
build forts, make out hard —
let's never sleep.
I love you.
I know you love me;
all I need.
This is hard!
I thought I understood you!
I’m not happy.
I’m not afraid,
but I don’t know what
I'm doing anymore.
Is this real?
The things we all do...
what we say.
Everything is changing.
Nothing ever makes sense;
not out here.
My little girl;
you’re a precious little thing
in my hands.
I like things:
especially things that I like.
Yes, those things.
Hello my son.
Are you ready to live?
I’ll be here.
I can help
again, I found a way
to help people.
I, us, we —
We have found a place
to be happy.

What is this?

Each year, on my birthday, I try to remember my entire life. I have compiled songs, taken photos, conducted interviews and composed letters, in a effort to remember the nuances of my Earthy experience so far. This year I wrote poetry. One poem for each year.

The lune is an American form of poetry similar to the haiku.  One way of writing a lune is to count the words: three words in the first line; five words in the second line; three words in the third line.

After writing the poems I plotted them on a graph indicating the relative happiness expressed in the poem. Fireworks lit up the base of my skull as I connected the dots and discovered a sort of happiness-graph. My life’s joy, laid out before me.

Creating this interactive experience based on the poems and the graph was a joy and I am very excited to share it with you.

Thank you for stopping by.

-T
