Introduction

Most of my time in NYC was spent running full tilt around the city, trying to take it all in. I was very aggressive in my sight-seeing. In doing so, I spent a lot of time in the streets, among the people. Overhearing their conversations, and being a part of what the city is. I think that was the best "sight" of the city. to be a part of it.

Radio City Music Hall

At one point this concert hall was the number one tourist desination of the city. Fact.

Brooklyn Bridge

New Yorkers speak of this bridge with pride, but I found it dirty and without a good view.

NYC Public Library

Made famous by ghostbusters, this library is flippin huge. Couldn't find the books tho.

Bryant Park

Wandered through this park on the way to the library, very nice place to eat a sandwich.

Stand Up Comedy

I had wanted to catch a show while I was in NY, but wrote it off. Then I met a scalper.

Times Square

The only place in the world more gaudy than the Las Vegas Strip. Another fact.

Mid Town comics

I would see ads for this place in my comics as a kid, turns out it's just another comic store.

the Dakota Building

Made famous for being the last home of John Lennon. He was shot and killed in front of it.

Empire State Building

I missed the lines and got to the top in about 10 minutes. Breathtaking view. Awesome.

Dumbo, Brooklyn

I don't think I saw this one right, I mostly wandered through the back streets, lost.

Central Park Ice Cream Truck

What better thing to do in Central Park than eat Ice Cream on a hot day?

Grand Centeral Terminal

I tried getting a straight shot of this place without getting run over. Nearly impossible.

5th ave Apple Store

This store is just amazing. A big glass cube in the middle of the city. Awesome glass elevator.

Highline Park

Came across this elevated park at sunset. It goes over streets and through buildings. Cool!

Manhattan bridge

It gets less love, but I enjoyed it more than the brooklyn bridge. better view and less people.

Meat Packing District

Where the young and hip live. The moment I stepped into this neighborhood I felt hip.

New Museum

Hailed as the successor to the MET. I went in, looked around, peed, and left. Too stuffy for me.

Centeral Park

One of the more enjoyable parts of the city. Very easy to get disoriented in it tho.

World trade Center

Very touching, and very clever. The foundations of the buldings are replaced w/ waterfalls.

Rockerfeller Center

Famously home of NBC studios. I loves the greek lore as art all around the property.

Grimaldi's Pizza

NY pizza is easy, fast, and cheap. Enjoyed early and often. Nom nom, here's two dollars.

Pier 62 Skatepark

Randomly came across a bunch of super cool cats skating it up in the city. Took awesome photos.

Strawberry Fields

At the foot of the Dakota, this area of Centeral Park is decorated with roses and tears.

Conclusion

When I was a kid I was told by an adult that I respected that I needed to go to New York at least once in my life. "There is an electricity in the streets that you can't find anywhere else on the planet" he told me. This sparked in me the desire to see it, to experience what he was talking about.

I jumped at the opportunity when it finally came to me. I attacked the city, taking in as much as I could. I wanted to be a part of the current. I did it, and I think I did it well. This is my report.
