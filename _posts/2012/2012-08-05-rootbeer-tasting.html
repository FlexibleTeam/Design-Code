Rootbeer Tasting

The careful craft of brewing does not belong to fermented drinks alone. Family recipies, exotic ingredients & regional tastes are all a part of the rootbeer culture, which I adore.

Dive In

I have always favored rootbeer as my choice frosty beverage. In this article I hand pick ten gormet brews and put them to the test, judging them for head, bite, body and aftertaste. Which is the best?

  • Abita
  • Bulldog
  • Death Valley
  • Firemans Brew
  • Frostie
  • Goose Island
  • Hanks
  • Margos Dark
  • Red Arrow
  • Rocket Fizz Float

Abita

Body

The body has sharp tastes of root beer spices, but is not sweet or very exciting due to the lack of honey or vanilla which could bring about a bit more creaminess.

Bite

The initial contact with the tongue is fairly weak, no explosion of flavor that you get with a good brew. The bite itself goes just as it comes, quickly and without celebration.

After Taste

The aftertaste feels like a rush of the flavor of the body leaving the mouth quickly, it doesn't really linger or offer anything different than the rest of the drink. A good aftertaste should be enjoyable, the best stuff from the body and bite saying goodbye to your mouth.

Head

A nice 1.5 inches tall.

Color #966f05

Kind of thin and watery.

Ingredients

Cane Sugar, "Root Beer Flavor"

Rating

I wouldn't pick it out of a lineup, but if you gave me one, I'd drink it

Bulldog

Body

The body is a wonderful combination of delicate flavors. The honey and vanilla work together to bring about a real sweetness within the root spices. I like cane sugar much better than high fructose corn syrup, it has a different mouth feel, but is mostly evidenced in the lack oh a too-heavy aftertaste.

Bite

In this brew the carbonation doesn't bite much. There is a significant bite from the spices, however. This excites me.

After Taste

The aftertaste is a soft flush of cool flavors. the cool spices and sweet honey and vanilla rest on the tongue for a few pleasurable seconds. a nice clean finish.

Head

Very little head that fizzled away.

Color #8e5d11

A rich sticky color.

Ingredients

Cane Sugar, Honey, Real Vanilla

Rating

I'll have another please.

Death Valley

Body

The body is closely tied to the bite except for the addition of Vanilla and an increase of tingle from the carbonation. The ingredients are simple and so is the flavor, yet very intense. A very full body indeed.

Bite

The initial bite is thick and heavy no to much carbonation, but a whole lotta spice flavor.

After Taste

The aftertaste is a smooth release from the heaviness of the body and a soft embrace of the vanilla who takes us home to finnish.

Head

Wow! A huge foamy three inches that lasted nearly 30seconds! Truly amazing.

Color #633b03

Old west dark.

Ingredients

Indian Wells Artisan Spring Water, Cane Sugar, Real Vanilla

Rating

A worthy beer.

Fireman's Brew

Body

Not too much carbonation, the natural flavors come through quite well. Heavy, full bodied, and not watery at all. A very pleasant mouthfeel, smooth and not hard to drink.

Bite

Strong bite, but after head goes away, you can taste more of the spices, and the bite lessens considerably.

After Taste

After taste lasts about 2 seconds long, it's fresh and not acidic at all. The sweetness stays on your tongue for a bit.

Head

2in before I had to stop pouring, lasted about 20 seconds, the head didn't have much flavor.

Color #852909

Deep brown, when lit it has a deep red.

Ingredients

Cane Sugar, Caramel Coloring

Rating

Nothing special, nothing wrong.

Frostie

Body

Although this is packaged as a "Vanilla Rootbeer", it is still very much a root beer flavor. A full bodied brew with a delightful blend of spices. with the additional smooth creaminess of vanilla.

Bite

Just what you want / expect in the bite. A strong punch of flavor and fizz that brings a very fun and lively mouth feel.

After Taste

The aftertaste is where the vanilla really takes center stage. The spice bite fizzes away in the mouth to leave a nice, creamy vanilla aftertaste. A wonderful taste all around however, in this root beer the aftertaste is the most exciting part.

Head

A quick fizzle, not much.

Color #8e5d11

A rich sticky color.

Ingredients

High Fructose Corn Syrup

Rating

I'll have another please.

Hanks

Body

Overall is very soft, a perfect blend of tingle and flavor. The spices combine well with there carbonation. A very well blended full body.

Bite

A very soft bite, yet creamy. Welcome to the flavors. The carbonation is light and second stage to the cream.

After Taste

A nice creamy aftertaste that does not linger too long, and does not go away too quickly. It's very sweet and simple.

Head

1.5 inches, but lasted for over 20 seconds. smooth and creamy. It says, "hello!" to your tasebuds.

Color #804218

Warm brown with a hint of deep red.

Ingredients

Cane sugar, Quillaia and Yucca Extracts, Acacia.

Rating

A calm yet refreshingly sweet brew

Margo's Bark

Body

The carbonation is a good balance of tingle and excitement, but also steps back to let this great list of flavors come through. The nutmeg and wintergreen are well voiced together in this brew and bring about an uncommon mixture of freshness and sweet warmth.

Bite

The initial bite is gentle an smooth the carbonation and spices hit evenly, welcoming your mouth to the experience.

After Taste

The after taste is one unique flavor after another coming at you in rapid succession, bound together my the creaminess of the vanilla, and retained for just-the-right amount of time by the sweet silky molasses.

Head

Nice frothy .5 inches tall.

Color #633b03

Dark indeed.

Ingredients

Evaporated Cane Juice, Molasses, Vanilla, Clove, Nutmeg, Wintergreen, Yucca Extract.

Rating

A Worthy Beer.

Red Arrow

Body

A very full body with a strong punch of carbonation and wintergreen. The licorice comes through pretty strong through the body making it very dark, I may be just quite thirsty, but this brew is quite refreshing.

Bite

A smooth, sizzley bite, I can taste the spices doing their work, the licorice and wintergreen doing their work after the carbonation dies down.

After Taste

The wintergreen comes through at the end, its a tingly sensation and a kind of freshness that is really unique.

Head

No head at all, none! Not that there's anything wrong with that...

Color #852909

Deep brown, when lit it has a deep red.

Ingredients

Cane Sugar, Wintergreen, Licorice

Rating

For a unique refreshing wintergreen finish.

Rocket Fizz

Body

Surprisingly, this is a fairly weak bodied brew (maybe because I just reviewed Margo's Bark, which was an excellent full bodied drink.) the spices are nice and flavorful, but lacks that special something. Positioned as a "root beer float" I would have liked more creaminess and some vanilla.

Bite

Not much of a bite, the brew comes in cold and fresh, but it doesn't really hit until it settles to the body. I like a spicy bubbly bite.

After Taste

The after taste is where the "float" is. There are feint traces of something like that, but if I wasn't looking for it I might have missed it.

Head

Good frothy head, quick fizzle. About 1.5 inches.

Color #d69416

Light brown.

Ingredients

Pure Cane Sugar

Rating

One is good enough.
